## Phase 3: Historical Patterns & Academic Research

### Historical Effects of Uncertainty and Inequality

**Source**: [Historical effects of shocks on inequality: the great leveller revisited](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41599-021-00763-4)

**Key Finding**:
> "In the twentieth century great shocks and disasters, most notably the 1929 economic crisis and the two World Wars, led to reduced wealth inequalities"

**BUT Critical Exception**:
> "Most historical disasters were rather followed by a widening of wealth gaps. As most societies were characterized by economic and political skewness, the result mostly was a further widening of disparities."

> "The financial collapse of 2008 became the first post-1900 crisis that pushed wealth inequality up. This could only happen because the societal setting had become markedly different from that in most of the twentieth century."

**What This Means:**
- **20th century pattern**: Total war ‚Üí inequality decreases (mass mobilization, capital destruction)
- **21st century pattern**: Financial crisis ‚Üí inequality increases (bailouts, asset inflation)
- **Implication**: We're in a NEW pattern where crises increase inequality instead of reducing it

**Source**: [Inequality: Total war as a great leveller](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/inequality-total-war-great-leveller)

**Finding**:
> "Mass mobilisation warfare that sometimes triggered radical revolution greatly narrowed the gap between elites and masses... The two world wars witnessed the most serious declines in returns on capital on record."

**Key Insight**:
- Inequality reduction historically required EXTREME violence/disruption
- Modern crises (2008, COVID) didn't have that leveling effect
- Question: Can inequality be reduced without catastrophic violence?

**Source**: [Wealth Inequality as a Catalyst for Civil War: Historical Patterns and Economic Thresholds](https://gcoleman86.substack.com/p/wealth-inequality-as-a-catalyst-for)

**Finding**:
> "Extreme inequality shows up in nearly every case of major crisis. When big gaps exist between the haves and have-nots, not just in material wealth but also access to positions of power, this breeds frustration, dissent and turmoil."

## Phase 4: AI Impact on Inequality - CRITICAL NUANCE

### The Surprising Finding: AI May REDUCE Wage Inequality

**Source**: [OECD - What Impact Has AI Had on Wage Inequality?](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2024/11/what-impact-has-ai-had-on-wage-inequality_8943dfe0/7fb21f59-en.pdf) (November 2024)

**Finding**:
> "While wage inequality has increased less (or reduced more) within the occupations most exposed to AI, there is no indication that AI has reduced wage gaps between men and women in those occupations, nor between young and prime age workers."

**Source**: [AI raises average wages by 21% and substantially reduces wage inequality, researchers find](https://www.foxbusiness.com/economy/ai-raises-average-wages-21-substantially-reduces-wage-inequality-researchers-find)

**Working Paper Finding**:
> "AI 'substantially reduces wage inequality while raising average wages by 21 percent,' with this equalizing effect fully driven by 'simplification,' enabling workers across skill levels to compete for the same jobs."

**Mechanism**:
> "Unlike previous waves of automation that increased both wage and wealth inequality, AI could reduce wage inequality through the displacement of high-income workers."

**Source**: [Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago - AI in the Office and the Factory](https://www.chicagofed.org/publications/chicago-fed-insights/2025/artificial-intelligence-in-office-factory)

**Key Distinction**:
> "In factory settings, AI increases employment of lower-skilled workers by making machines more productive and easier to operate, and because this effect dominates, AI increases employment and decreases inequality."

**BUT - The Wealth Inequality Catch**:
> "High-income workers' tasks appear highly complementary with AI, potentially increasing their productivity, and they are better positioned to benefit from higher capital returns, with wealth inequality effects particularly pronounced when firms can choose adoption rates."

**Source**: [IMF - AI Adoption and Inequality](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2025/04/04/ai-adoption-and-inequality-565729)

**Nuance**:
> "Workers with skills that enable them to use AI may see their productivity and wages increase, whereas those who do not may see their wages decrease."

**Within-Group Findings**:
> "Several studies found that within certain occupation groups‚Äîincluding lawyers, software engineers, customer service agents, management consultants, and workers performing professional writing tasks‚Äîthe lowest skilled or least experienced workers derive much greater productivity gains from AI than their higher skilled, more experienced counterparts."

**Policy Implication**:
> "Training policies could benefit from targeting lower-skilled workers who are able to use AI, given the greater productivity benefits for low performers."

### How AI Is Different from Previous Automation

**Source**: [NBER - Artificial Intelligence and the Skill Premium](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w32430/w32430.pdf)

**Traditional Pattern**:
> "For decades, technology has been 'skill-biased': It substituted for routine skills common in middle- and some low-wage jobs (such as manual accounting, production, and food preparation), while it complemented non-routine skills typical of higher-paid jobs (such as managerial decisionmaking, complex analysis, and the use of human creativity)."

**AI's Reversal**:
> "AI is a technology designed to substitute for non-routine and skill-intensive tasks such that it is a better substitute for high-skill workers than traditional physical capital and a better substitute for high-skill workers than for low-skill workers."

**Source**: [Brookings - Generative AI, the American worker, and the future of work](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/generative-ai-the-american-worker-and-the-future-of-work/)

**New Reality**:
> "Generative AI excels at mimicking the kinds of non-routine skills and interactive traits that just a few years ago experts considered impossible for computers to perform, including programming, prediction, writing, creativity, projecting empathy, communication and persuasion, and analysis."

> "The labor market transformation brought on by AI and artificial general intelligence (AGI) will not look like earlier iterations of general-purpose technologies (GPTs). Previous GPTs ushered in an era of 'skills-biased technological change,' sparking the four-year college revolution and the advancement of STEM education policy. AI and AGI could instead democratize what had been high-skilled technical tasks, and redefine 'high-skill' and high-wage jobs."

**CRITICAL IMPLICATION**:
- Historical advice: "Get a college degree, learn high-skill work" ‚Üí protected from automation
- AI era: High-skill work is MOST exposed
- This is unprecedented

## Phase 8: Media Bias & Perception of Uncertainty

**Source**: [The Scary World Syndrome: News Orientations, Negativity Bias, and the Cultivation of Anxiety](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/15205436.2023.2297829)

**Finding**:
> "Isolated and negative events are mistakenly presented as daily reality due to the infrequency of incidents paradoxically explaining their level of news coverage. Media's tendency to present the negative and exceptional as reality can cultivate a distorted worldview among audiences."

**Source**: [Cross-national evidence of a negativity bias in psychophysiological reactions to news](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1908369116)

**Evidence**:
> "Evidence suggests that, all around the world, the average human is more physiologically activated by negative than by positive news stories."

**Source**: [Mediatization and the Disproportionate Attention to Negative News](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/1461670X.2018.1423632)

**Historical Trend**:
> "Scholars have argued that, as a consequence of increased commercial pressures on news media institutions, media's penchant for the negative and the exceptional has become more prominent over time, with factors such as negativity growing to become among the most dominant selection criteria of today's media logics."

**Consequences**:
> "The negative bias in contemporary news reporting comes with the risk of undesirable consequences, such as biased perceptions of reality, lower mental well-being, and news avoidance. Negatively biased coverage can induce irrational fear perceptions, lower general well-being, and increase support for radical right-wing politics."

**IMPLICATION**:
- Our PERCEPTION of uncertainty may be higher than reality
- BUT: Real uncertainty (job loss, climate, geopolitics) is also objectively high
- Question: How much of anxiety is warranted vs. media-induced?

### Antifragility & Optionality (Nassim Taleb)

**Source**: [Nassim Taleb on the Anti-Fragile Portfolio](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/articles/2013/05/14/nassim-taleb-on-the-anti-fragile-portfolio-and-the-benefits-of-taking-risks)

**Core Concept**:
> "Antifragile describes phenomena that gain from 'the disorder cluster' - namely volatility, uncertainty, disturbances, randomness, and stressors."

**Source**: [Nassim Taleb's Optionality: Unlimited Gains With Limited Costs](https://fronterabrands.com/optionality-taleb/)

**Optionality Principle**:
> "The more options you have, the more freedom you have to respond to unforeseen circumstances, and the less fragile you are to sudden events."

> "Optionality reduces your dependence on predictions and information because with an option, you can decide after the events occurred."

**Source**: [How To Build An Antifragile Career](https://www.fastcompany.com/3003416/how-build-antifragile-career)

**Career Application**:
> "Develop diverse skills, pursue optionality, and avoid over-specialization."

**Barbell Strategy**:
> "Have a steady income but work on side projects with unlimited upside - you are safe with a guaranteed income, but you also create optionality; most of your side projects will fail, but one successful project will be enough to change your life."

**PRACTICAL TRANSLATION**:
- Don't try to predict the future
- Build systems that benefit from uncertainty
- Career barbell: Safe base income + high-upside experiments
- Optionality > optimization
